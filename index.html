Dr. Rui Zhang is an Assistant Professor in the Computer Science and Engineering Department of Penn State University and a co-director of the PSU NLP Lab. His research interest lies in Natural Language Processing, Machine Learning, and Artificial Intelligence. He has published more than 20 papers at leading NLP/ML/AI conferences. He co-organized the Interactive and Executable Semantic Parsing workshop at EMNLP 2020 and serves as Area Chair at NAACL 2021, EMNLP 2021, NLPCC 2021. He is one of the recipients of 2020 Amazon Research Awards. He received B.S. degrees from both Shanghai Jiao Tong University and the University of Michigan in 2015 and received his Ph.D. from Computer Science Department at Yale University in 2020. During his Ph.D., he has done research internships at IBM Thomas J. Watson Research Center, Grammarly Research, and Google AI.

Research Interests

I am broadly interested in Natural Language Processing, Machine Learning, and Artificial Intelligence, with special focus on

  • Semantic Parsing and Natural Language Interfaces (Text-to-SQL, Conversational Semantic Parsing, Table-based Question Answering)
  • Text Generation and Summarization (Data-to-Text Generation, Dialogue Summarization)
  • Multilingual Information Access (Multilingual Information Retrieval, Cross-Lingual Semantic Parsing)
  • Efficient and Generalizable NLP Models (Few-shot NLP, Network Sparsity and Efficient Training)
